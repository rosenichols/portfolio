About the Society

The Waxbill Finch Society was formed in 1991 and its primary concern is with the keeping and breeding of African and Asiatic small finches.

The society is affiliated with the National Council for Aviculture through membership of the Foreign Bird Federation. An account of birds bred by society members is kept in order that the status of the species in British aviculture can be ascertained.

Our magazine, The Waxbill, is issued quarterly to all members and contains news and articles on all matters relating to the keeping and breeding of birds.

A total of 63 species of bird are covered by the society, and members are encouraged to endeavour to breed all birds that they keep in order to ensure that birds are available for future generations.

To spearhead the organisation of breeding colonies, many species of bird are focussed on by a dedicated species co-ordinator, whom will no doubt have a lot of valuable advise on methods of breeding.
